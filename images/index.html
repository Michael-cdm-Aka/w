Lamborghini's History

Lamborghini is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963. The company initially started as a tractor manufacturer but later expanded into the automotive industry to compete with established brands like Ferrari. Today, Lamborghini is a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group, operating under the Audi division.

Models

Throughout its history, Lamborghini has produced several iconic models, such as the Miura, Countach, Diablo, and Murciélago. These cars are known for their distinctive design, powerful engines, and limited production runs, making them highly sought after by car collectors worldwide.

Motorsports

Lamborghini has a rich motorsports history, participating in various racing series, including Formula 1, sports car racing, and rallying. The company also has its one-make racing series, the Lamborghini Super Trofeo, which features identical Huracán Super Trofeo EVO cars competing in regional championships worldwide.

Limited Edition and Special Series

In addition to its regular model lineup, Lamborghini frequently releases limited edition and special series cars, such as the Aventador SVJ, Sian, and Centenario. These cars often feature unique design elements, enhanced performance, and exclusive specifications, making them highly desirable among Lamborghini enthusiasts.
